The History of Stormageddon Meatball

First, you might be wondering why we called one of our cats Stormageddon Meatball it's kind of a strange name.
Well we didn't adopt him from the animal shelter. He was a stray cat. It all happened when we were eating meatballs for dinner and we heard a cat meowing outside during a storm. We let it inside. Because we didn't have a cat so we had no cat food but we did have... Meatballs! So we gave him the meatballs and he loved them. But he was was really skinny so he was desperate for food. He loved snuggling, so my brother and I of course asked to keep him. My mom's response was not yes or no; it was, "Maybe". But my brother and I loved cats so we took that as a yes. And we took him the to vet the next day in a cat carrier that was half his size. He was so skinny that he could fit into it just by crouching. We decided to keep him, and the vet gave us a new carrier. Now we had to think of a name for him. So we thought of Stormy, because it was a stormy night when he showed up. Then we thought of Doctor Who and named him Stormageddon. And he ate meatballs, so Stormageddon Meatball it was! And it still is.
The End

The History of Myrna Pepperoni

It all begins with a facebook post, where an adorable grey kitten was looking for a home. My dad really wanted to bring this kitten home. But he was not the only one who felt that way, and someone else got the kitten before him. But the woman who was trying to find it a home kept an eye out for a different cat for him. A few weeks later, she came back and told him about another kitty. She described the cat as, "black, very loving, calm, and petite". He was interested. The next day, there came a cuddley picture and a video. About five days later, he went to an animal clinic in Dayton to pick her up and bring her home for the night. Myrna spent about 2 hours exploring his apartment, after which she got up onto the couch with him, leaned against his hip and fell asleep. The next day he decided to keep her.
